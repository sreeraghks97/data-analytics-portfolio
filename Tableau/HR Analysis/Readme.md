# 👔 HR Analysis Dashboard

An interactive Tableau dashboard designed to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, job roles, and income distribution.  
The dashboard provides insights into employee turnover patterns, workforce composition, and potential retention challenges, supporting HR decision-making and talent management strategies.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The objective of this project is to explore HR employee data and identify patterns in attrition across departments, job roles, age groups, and income categories.

By visualizing workforce trends and attrition drivers, this dashboard helps understand factors associated with employee turnover and highlights areas where organizations can focus their retention efforts.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns across departments and job roles.
- Evaluate attrition trends across different age and income groups.
- Understand the relationship between career growth opportunities and employee retention.
- Provide interactive visualizations for deeper workforce analysis.

---

## 📂 Dataset

**Dataset:** HR Employee Records

The dataset includes employee-level information such as:

- Employee ID
- Age
- Gender
- Department
- Job Role
- Income Group
- Attrition Status
- Promotion History

---

## 🛠 Tools & Techniques

- **Tableau Desktop**
- Bar Charts for department and role-wise attrition analysis
- Scatter Plots for promotion vs. attrition analysis
- KPI Cards for workforce metrics
- Filters and Parameters for interactive exploration
- Data Visualization and Dashboard Storytelling

---

## 📊 Dashboard Components

### 1. Attrition by Department & Role
- Visualizes employee turnover distribution across different departments and job roles.
- Helps identify areas with higher employee exits.

### 2. Age Group Analysis
- Highlights attrition patterns across different employee age groups.
- Helps understand workforce retention challenges among various demographics.

### 3. Income Group Impact
- Analyzes attrition trends across different income categories.
- Helps identify possible relationships between compensation levels and employee turnover.

### 4. Promotion vs. Attrition Analysis
- Displays the relationship between career progression opportunities and employee retention.
- Helps understand whether promotion opportunities may influence employee decisions.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- The **Sales department** recorded the highest attrition compared to other departments.
- Younger employees showed higher attrition rates, indicating possible retention challenges among early-career employees.
- Higher attrition was observed among lower-income groups, suggesting a possible relationship between compensation levels and employee turnover.
- Employees with fewer career advancement opportunities showed higher likelihood of leaving.

---

## 💼 Business Recommendations

- HR teams can focus targeted retention strategies on departments with higher attrition rates, such as Sales.
- Organizations can improve employee engagement among younger employees through mentorship programs and career development opportunities.
- Reviewing compensation structures and benefits for lower-income groups may help improve retention.
- Strengthening promotion pathways and internal growth opportunities can support long-term employee satisfaction.
